Splitting a Palazzo into Luxury Apartments: Cadastral Procedures: the difference is made by concrete technical choices, not generic statements.

1. Cadastral aspects

Subdivision requires precise cadastral updates. Each unit must have clear boundaries. Minimum surface requirements must be met. Records must match the real condition. Cadastral errors affect future sales. Technical and legal parts are inseparable.

2. MEP and access

Each unit needs separate or metered systems. Independent access improves value. Common routes must comply with safety codes. MEP changes can be invasive. Integrated design is required. MEP planning is critical.

ðŸ’¡ Pro Tip: Plan separable systems early; it simplifies approvals and increases value.

3. Common parts and rules

Common parts must be defined clearly. A clear condominium regulation is required. Shared stairs, lifts, and systems affect costs. Technical spaces must be planned. Clear management avoids disputes. Value depends on good management.
