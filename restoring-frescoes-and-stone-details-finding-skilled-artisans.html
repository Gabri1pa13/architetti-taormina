This guide gets practical about Restoring Frescoes and Stone Details: Finding Skilled Artisans, highlighting what to verify before decisions and what to avoid on site.

1. Diagnosis and survey

Fresco restoration starts with detailed damage mapping. Causes may include moisture, salts, or micro-cracking. Without diagnosis you treat symptoms only. Photographic documentation is essential. Qualified conservators must be involved early. Diagnosis is half the job.

2. Conservative techniques

Restoration must be conservative and reversible. Cleaning must avoid removing original layers. Integrations should be compatible and recognizable. Materials must allow breathability. Aggressive work lowers heritage value. Conservation outweighs immediate aesthetics.

3. Microclimate control

Humidity and temperature control are essential for durability. HVAC should avoid thermal shocks. Ventilation must be steady but not aggressive. Stable microclimate reduces new cracking. Maintenance is part of the project. Without prevention, restoration does not last.
