This guide gets practical about Structural Consolidation of 18th Century Buildings: Techniques and Costs, highlighting what to verify before decisions and what to avoid on site.

1. Structural assessment

Historic floors and vaults require precise surveys and material tests. Existing deflections reveal critical weaknesses. Without diagnosis, reinforcement can be excessive or ineffective. A correct structural model guides decisions. The assessment is non-negotiable. It defines the whole strategy.

2. Reinforcement techniques

Reinforcements must be compatible with original materials. FRP, ties, and collaborating slabs must be chosen case by case. The aim is to improve capacity without adding heavy loads. Reversibility is often required in heritage contexts. Wrong solutions can compromise global behavior. Compatibility is more important than strength.

ðŸ’¡ Pro Tip: Use light, distributed reinforcements to improve behavior without overloading the structure.

3. Site control

Consolidation requires continuous site control. Phasing must prevent temporary instability. Safety of workers and structure is critical. Installation quality defines final performance. Changes must be validated by the site supervisor. Control is part of the result.
